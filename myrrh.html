Commiphora myrrha

Contents:

a-pinene, cadinene, limonene, cuminaldehyde, eugenol, m-cresol, heerabolene, acetic acid, formic acid and other sesquiterpenes and acids.

Properties:

anti-catarrhal, anti-inflammatory, antimicrobial, antiphlogistic, antiseptic, astringent, balsamic, carminative, cicatrisant, emmenagogue, expectorant, fungicidal, sedative, digestive and pulmonary stimulant, stomachic, tonic, uterine, vulnerary

Uses:

Diffused; colds, catarrh, coughs, sore throats, bronchitis, excessive mucus. During childbirth it can help ease pain.

Topically; boils, skin ulcers, bed sores, chapped and cracked skin, ringworm, weeping wounds, eczema, athlete's foot. It can also be used as part of a mouthwash for most problems in the mouth.

Cautions:

Increases blood flow to the uterus. Do not use while while pregnant.

Myrrh has known drug interactions with diabetes medications. Warfarin (Coumadin) is specifically known to interact with Myrrh and is likely to reduce effectiveness of the medication. Mayo clinic

First aid:

If skin irritation has occurred apply cornstarch or similar to absorb excess.

If gotten in the eye rinse with clear water for 15 min, if irritation persists for more than 30 min seek professional help.

If a pregnant woman has been exposed monitor closely for signs of labour or miscarriage.
