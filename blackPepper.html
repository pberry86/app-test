Piper nigrum

Contents:

a-thujone, a-pinene, camphene, sabinene, b-pinene, a-phellandrene, myrcene, limonene, caryophyllene, b-farnesene, b-bisabolene, linalool and terpinen-4-ol

Properties:

analgesic, antiseptic, antispasmodic, antitoxic, aphrodisiac, diaphoretic, digestive, diuretic, febrifuge, laxative, rubefacient, tonic (especially of the spleen)

Uses:

Topical; pain relief, rheumatism, chills, flu, colds, increase circulation, exhaustion, muscular aches,encourages peristalsis, fever.

Cautions:

Skin sensitizing if used undiluted. Do not apply to the face, effects could be similar to pepper spray.

Best to avoid diffusing.

First aid:

If gotten in the eye rinse with clear water for 15 min and seek professional help.

If contact burns or sensitization occurs apply flour or cornstarch to absorb excess oil.
