Laurus nobilis

Contains:

1,8-CINEOLE, 4-TERPINEOL, ACETYL-EUGENOL, ALPHA-PHELLANDRENE, ALPHA-PINENE, ALPHA-TERPINENE, ALPHA-TERPINEOL, BETA-ELEMENE, BETA-EUDESMOL, BETA-MYRCENE, BETA-PINENE, BORNEOL, CAMPHENE, CAMPHOR, CARVACROL, DELTA-CADINENE, ESTRAGOLE, EUGENOL, EUGENOL-ACETATE, EUGENOL-METHYL-ETHER, GAMMA-TERPINENE, GERANIOL, HUMULENE, LIMONENE, LINALOOL, MYRCENE, P-CYMENE, SABINENE, TERPINEN-4-OL, THYMOL, TRANS-PINOCARVEOL

Properties:

Antiseptic, antibiotic, analgesic, anti-neuralgic, antispasmodic, aperitif, astringent, emmenagogue, febrifuge, insecticide, cholagogue, sedative, stomachic, sudorific and tonic.

Uses:

Topically;

rheumatic pains, excess gas.

Diffused;

calming, headaches. Low exposure can be stimulating while heavy exposure is sedating.

Cautions:

Do not use if pregnant.

May be a skin irritant.

Ensure good dilution even when diffusing.

First aid:

If skin irritation has occurred apply cornstarch or similar to absorb excess.

If gotten in the eye rinse with clear water for 15 min, if irritation persists for more than 30 min seek professional help.

If a pregnant woman has been exposed monitor closely for signs of labour or miscarriage.
