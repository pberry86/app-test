Melissa officinalis

Contains:

TRANS-OCIMENE, CIS-OCIMENE, 3-OCTANONE, METHYL HEPENONE, CIS-3-HEXENOL, 3-OCTANOL, 1-OCTEN-3-OL, COPAENE, CITRONELLAL, LINALOOL, B-BOURBONENE, CARYOPHYLLENE, A-HUMULENE, NERAL, GERMACRENE-D, GERANIAL, GERANYL ACETATE, D-CADINENE, Y-CADINENE, NEROL, GERANIOL

Properties:

Antidepressant, antispasmodic, bactericidal, carminative, cordial, diaphoretic, emmenagogue, febrifuge, hypotensive, nervine, sedative, stomachic, sudorific, tonic.

Uses:

Diffused;

Depression, calming, headaches, nausea, stress.

Topically;

Fever, upset stomach, fungal infections.

Cautions:

Oil can cause sensitization, high dilution is best.

Pregnant women should avoid due to emmenagogue properties.

First aid:

If skin irritation has occurred apply cornstarch or similar to absorb excess.

If gotten in the eye rinse with clear water for 15 min, if irritation persists for more than 30 min seek professional help.

If a pregnant woman has been exposed monitor closely for signs of labour or miscarriage.
