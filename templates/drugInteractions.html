Drug interactions are not something most people think about, particularly in regards to essential oils. However they can and do occur.

In most cases the route of exposure being the same will increase the odds of an interaction dramatically. For example inhaled medications will be more likely to interact with diffused oils.

Topical medications have a risk of interaction with topically applied oils in the same area.

Some oils have effects on blood sugar and blood pressure. In both cases the potential for drug interactions that also effect these things exists.

Oils that have a balancing effect on the hormones can interact with hormonal medications (eg. hormonal birth control, fertility treatments).

A drug interaction is not guaranteed however it is wise to know that it is possible. At this time no profession has training on how to properly handle drug/natural medicine interactions.
