The second, is to remember that the smaller the body the faster a high concentration is reached. For example proper dosing of pharmaceutical medications is done based on body weight. The same principle applies with any form of natural medicine.

With small animals that live in a tank or cage no essential oils should be used around them at all.

Cats lack a liver enzyme called glucuronyl transferase which makes all essential oils potentially toxic to the point of being lethal. Only hydrosols should be used around them.

Dogs have a little more room with essential oils, however direct application should be avoided 99% of the time (eg active infection, still dilute). Diffusing occasionally is ok.

If there is a flea issue you want to deal with naturally, get neem oil and dilute it in a tear free baby shampoo. Neem can kill all life stages of fleas, lice, mites, ect. If some residue is accidentally left in the fur the animal will not be harmed when grooming themselves.
