Boswellia Serrata

Contents:

a-pinene, actanol, linalool, octyl acetate, bornyl acetate, incensole and incensyl acetate

Properties:

antiseptic, astringent, carminative, cicatrisant, cytophylactic, digestive, diuretic, emmenagogue, expectorant, sedative, tonic, uterine, vulnerary and expectorant

Uses:

Diffused;

anxiety, relieves obsessive states of mind, opens the lungs, shortness of breath, asthma, bronchitis, laryngitis, coughs, colds.

Topically;

sores, carbuncles, wounds, scars, skin inflammation, painful menstruation, poor circulation, rheumatism, chilliness.

Cautions:

Boswellia may decrease the effects of pharmaceutical anti-inflammatories.

Pregnant individuals should avoid due to emmenagogue properties.

First aid:

If skin irritation has occurred apply cornstarch or similar to absorb excess.

If gotten in the eye rinse with clear water for 15 min, if irritation persists for more than 30 min seek professional help.

If a pregnant woman has been exposed monitor closely for signs of labour or miscarriage.
