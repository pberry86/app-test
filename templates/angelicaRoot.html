Angelica archangelica (root)

Contains:

ALPHA-HUMULENE, ALPHA-PHELLANDRENE, ALPHA-PINENE, ALPHA-TERPINENE, ANGELICIN, BERGAPTEN, BETA-BISABOLENE, BETA-ELEMENE, BETA-EUDESMOL, BETA-PHELLANDRENE, BETA-PINENE, BORNEOL, CAMPHENE, CARYOPHYLLENE, CIS-OCIMENE, CUPARENE, DELTA-CADINENE, ELEMOL, ETHYL-MYRISTATE, FENCHONE, GAMMA-TERPINENE, GERMACRENE-D, ISOPIMPINELLIN, LAURIC-ACID-ETHYL-ESTER, LIMONENE, LINALOOL, MYRCENE, OCTYL-ISOVALERATE, OLEIC-ACID-ETHYL-ESTER, OSTHOL, P-CYMENE, PALMITIC-ACID-ETHYL-ESTER, SABINENE, TERPINEN-4-OL, TERPINOLENE, TRANS-CARVEOL

Properties:

Antispasmodic, depurative, digestive, expectorant, hepatoprotective, nervine, tonic, antistress, detoxicant, diuretic, febrifuge, immune stimulant, stimulant, carminative, diaphoretic, emmenagogue, hepatic, liver tonic, stomachic

Uses:

Topically;

to ease pain from menstruation, poor digestion, headaches, and muscular pain.

Diffused;

helps relieve stress and fatigue, opens the lungs for better breathing.

Cautions:

Avoid during pregnancy.

Diabetics should also avoid this oil.

Oil is photo-toxic, avoid direct sun exposure for 24hrs.

First aid:

In the unlikely event of a skin reaction apply cornstarch or similar to absorb excess oil.

If gotten in the eyes rinse with clear water for 15 min, if irritation persists after 30 min seek professional help.

If a pregnant woman has been exposed monitor closely for signs of miscarriage.

If ingested call emergency services immediately.
