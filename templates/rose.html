Rosa damascene

Contains:

citronellol, geraniol, nerol, linalool, phenyl ethyl alcohol, farnesol, stearoptene, α-pinene, β-pinene, α-terpinene, limonene, p-cymene,camphene, β-caryophyllene, neral, citronellyl acetate, geranyl acetate, neryl acetate, eugenol, methyl eugenol, rose oxide, α-damascenone, β-damascenone, benzaldehyde, benzyl alcohol, rhodinyl acetate and phenyl ethyl formate.

Properties:

antidepressant, antispasmodic, aphrodisiac, astringent, antibacterial, antiviral, antiseptic, anti-inflammatory, hemostatic, cleansing, stomachic, expectorant, cholagogue, kidney tonic, emmenagogue, nervine, hepatic, laxative, cicatrisant, depurative, antiphlogistic

Uses:

Diffused; regulation of hormones, headaches, depression, anxiety.

Topical; scars, stretch marks, infection, scrapes, minor cuts, spasms, pain.

Cautions:

Pure rose absolute/otto must be diluted.

Pregnant individuals must avoid.

First aid:

If skin irritation has occurred apply cornstarch or similar to absorb excess.

If gotten in the eye rinse with clear water for 15 min, if irritation persists for more than 30 min seek professional help.

If a pregnant woman has been exposed monitor closely for signs of labour or miscarriage.
