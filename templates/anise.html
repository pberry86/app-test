Pimpinella anisum

Contains:

ALPHA-PINENE, ALPHA-TERPINENE, ANETHOLE, ANISALDEHYDE, ANISIC-ACID, BETA-BISABOLENE, BETA-ELEMENE, CAMPHENE, CAMPHOR, CARVONE, CHAMAZULENE, CINNAMALDEHYDE, CINNAMYL-ALCOHOL, CIS-ANETHOLE, ESTRAGOLE, EUGENOL, FENCHONE, LIMONENE, LINALOOL, PHELLANDRENE, SAFROLE, TERPINEOL, THYMOL, TRANS-ANETHOLE

Properties:

Antiasthmatic, anti-anxiety, anti-rheumatic, antiseptic, antispasmodic, aperitive, bronchial relaxant, carminative, decongestant, digestive, diuretic, expectorant, galactagogue, insecticidal, laxative, antiparasitic, sedative, stimulant

Uses:

Topically;

muscle pain and cramps, gas, difficult menstruation.

Diffused;

anxiety, difficulty breathing, headaches, nausea.

Cautions:

Can irritate skin so must always be properly diluted, also phototoxic, avoid sun exposure for 24hrs.

Those with liver disease and cancer should avoid this oil.

Excess use may cause dizziness and stomach ache.

Do not use if pregnant.

First aid:

If skin irritation has occurred apply cornstarch or similar to absorb excess.

If gotten in the eye rinse with clear water for 15 min, if irritation persists for more than 30 min seek professional help.

If a pregnant woman has come in direct contact monitor closely.
