Myrtus Communis

Contains:

ALPHA-PINENE, BETA-PINENE, LIMONENE, CINEOL, ALPHA-TERPINEN-4-OL, MYRTENOL, GERANIOL, LINALYL ACETATE, MYRTENYL ACETATE, CARVACROL

Properties:

Anticatarrhal, antiseptic, astringent, bactericidal, expectorant, balsamic.

Uses:

Diffused;

Phlegm, cattarh, sleeping aid, self destructive behavior, anger, greed, envy, fear.

Topically;

Acne, nervous tension, stress, piles, infection.

Cautions:

Excessive use can cause headaches and nausea.

First aid:

If skin irritation has occurred apply cornstarch or similar to absorb excess.

If gotten in the eye rinse with clear water for 15 min, if irritation persists for more than 30 min seek professional help.
