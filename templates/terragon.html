Atremisia dracunculus

Contains:

ESTRAGOLE, OCIMENE, PHELLANDRENE, CINEOL

Properties:

Anti-rheumatic, aperitif, digestive, deodorant, emmenagogue, stimulant, vermifuge.

Uses:

Diffused;

Digestion, menstruation.

Topically;

Anorexia, dyspepsia, flatulence, hiccups, internal spasm, nervous indigestion, menstrual complaints.

Cautions:

Pregnant women should avoid due to emmenagogue properties.

Estragole can be toxic, use oil in moderation.

First aid:

If skin irritation has occurred apply cornstarch or similar to absorb excess.

If gotten in the eye rinse with clear water for 15 min, if irritation persists for more than 30 min seek professional help.

If a pregnant woman has been exposed monitor closely for signs of labour or miscarriage.
