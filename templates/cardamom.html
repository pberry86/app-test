Elettaria cardomomum

Contains:

A-PINENE, B-PINENE, SABINENE, MYRCENE, A-PHELLANDRENE, LIMONENE, 1,8-CINEOLE, Y-TERPINENE, P-CYMENE, TERPINOLENE, LINALOOL, LINALYL ACETATE, TERPINEN-4-OIL, A-TERPINEOL, A-TERPINEOL ACETATE, CITRONELLOL, NEROL, GERANIOL, METHYL EUGENOL, TRANS-NEROLIDOL

Properties:

Antiseptic, antispasmodic, carminative, cephalic, digestive, diuretic, expectorant, stimulant, stomachic, tonic.

Uses:

Diffused;

Mental fatigue, coughs, phlegm, chest infections

Topically;

Laxative, colic, wind, dyspepsia, nausea, heartburn

Cautions:

No specific cautions.

First aid:

If skin irritation has occurred apply cornstarch or similar to absorb excess.

If gotten in the eye rinse with clear water for 15 min, if irritation persists for more than 30 min seek professional help.
