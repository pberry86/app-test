Hydrosols (also called hydrolats) are the water components left over from the steam distillation process for many essential oils.

Essential oils that are not created with steam or hydrodistillation will not have a hydrosol counterpart. There are also hydrosols which do not have an essential oil, witch hazel is one such example.

These water based co-products do contain some essential oil in them. Typically at a concentration of 0.01% to 0.04%, this ultra low concentration is why they are considered safe for babies and animals.

Hydrosols can be misted around a room as a spray or put into a diffuser. They can also be added to bath water and/or home made cosmetic products.

There is a small selection that have cautions attached to them, however it appears to largely be with internal use.

For seizures avoid Labrador Tea/Greenland moss.

For cancer avoid Bay leaf/Bay laurel.

For children under 2 avoid Sweet Gale/Bog Myrtle.

For kidney disease avoid Cypress,Goldenrod, and Juniper Berry.

For pregnancy avoid Rosemary, Sage, Cypress, Juniper Berry, Greenland Moss/Labrador Tea, Sweet Gale/Bog Myrtle, and Cornflower/Bachelor's Button.
